Intermediate Low Can-Do Statements

66 Points

The NCSSFL-ACTFL Can-Do Statements are self-assessment checklists used by language learners to assess what they “can do” with language in the Interpersonal, Interpretive, and Presentational modes of communication. Ultimately, the goal for all language learners is to develop a functional use of another language for one’s personal contexts and purposes. Use the checklist that will follow in a series of quizzes to record what you think you can do in the target language. You may realize that your progression may not be the same for each mode: Interpersonal, Interpretive, or Presentational. This is to be completely expected. For example, you may progress more quickly in Interpretive Reading than in Interpersonal Communication. To begin, you'll start the process with the lowest level of proficiency and work your way up from there. Make sure to be honest with yourself so you can best assess your own abilities in your target language.

Interpersonal Communication: Intermediate Low

I can participate in conversations on a number of familiar topics using simple sentences. I can handle short social interactions in everyday situations by asking and answering simple questions.

Q1

I can have a simple conversation on a number of everyday topics.

Q2

I can ask and answer questions on factual information that is familiar to me.

Q3

I can use the language to meet my basic needs in familiar situations.

Presentational Speaking: Intermediate Low

I can present information on most familiar topics using a series of simple sentences.

Q4

I can talk about people, activities, events, and experiences.

Q5

I can express my needs and wants.

Q6

I can present information on plans, instructions, and directions.

Q7

I can present songs, short skits, or dramatic readings.

Q8

I can express my preferences on topics of interest.

Presentational Writing: Intermediate Low

I can write briefly about most familiar topics and present information using a series of simple sentences.

Q9

I can write about people, activities, events, and experiences

Q10

I can prepare materials for a presentation.

Q11

I can write about topics of interest.

Q12

I can write basic instructions on how to make or do something.

Q13

I can write questions to obtain information.

Interpretive Listening: Intermediate Low

I can understand the main idea in short, simple messages and presentations on familiar topics. I can understand the main idea of simple conversations that I overhear.

Q14

I can understand the basic purpose of a message.

Q15

I can understand messages related to my basic needs.

Q16

I can understand questions and simple statements on everyday topics when I am part of the conversation.

Interpretive Reading: Intermediate Low

I can understand the main idea of short and simple texts when the topic is familiar.

Q17

I can understand messages in which the writer tells or asks me about topics of general interest.

Q18

I can identify some simple information needed on forms.

Q19

I can identify some information from news media.
