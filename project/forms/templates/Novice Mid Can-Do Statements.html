Novice Mid Can-Do Statements

76 Points

The NCSSFL-ACTFL Can-Do Statements are self-assessment checklists used by language learners to assess what they “can do” with language in the Interpersonal, Interpretive, and Presentational modes of communication. Ultimately, the goal for all language learners is to develop a functional use of another language for one’s personal contexts and purposes. Use the checklist that will follow in a series of quizzes to record what you think you can do in the target language. You may realize that your progression may not be the same for each mode: Interpersonal, Interpretive, or Presentational. This is to be completely expected. For example, you may progress more quickly in Interpretive Reading than in Interpersonal Communication. To begin, you'll start the process with the lowest level of proficiency and work your way up from there. Make sure to be honest with yourself so you can best assess your own abilities in your target language.

Interpersonal Communication: Novice Mid

I can communicate on very familiar topics using a variety of words and phrases that I have practiced or memorized.

Q1

I can greet and leave people in a polite way.

Q2

I can introduce myself and others.

Q3

I can answer a variety of simple questions.

Q4

I can make some simple statements in a conversation.

Q5

I can ask some simple questions.

Q6

I can communicate basic information about myself and people I know.

Q7

I can communicate some basic information about my everyday life.

Presentational Speaking: Novice Mid

I can present information about myself and some other very familiar topics using a variety of words, phrases, and memorized expressions.

Q8

I can present information about myself and others using words and phrases.

Q9

I can express my likes and dislikes using words, phrases, and memorized expressions.

Q10

I can present information about familiar items in my immediate environment.

Q11

I can talk about my daily activities using words, phrases, and memorized expressions.

Q12

I can present simple information about something I learned using words, phrases, and memorized expressions.

Presentational Writing: Novice Mid

I can write lists and memorized phrases on familiar topics.

Q13

I can fill out a simple form with some basic personal information.

Q14

I can write about myself using learned phrases and memorized expressions.

Q15

I can list my daily activities and write lists that help me in my day-to-day life.

Q16

I can write notes about something I have learned using lists, phrases, and memorized expressions.

Interpretive Listening: Novice Mid

I can recognize some familiar words and phrases when I hear them spoken.

Q17

I can understand a few courtesy phrases.

Q18

I can recognize and sometimes understand basic information in words and phrases I have memorized.

Q19

I can recognize and sometimes understand words and phrases that I have learned for specific purposes.

Interpretive Reading: Novice Mid

I can recognize some letters or characters. I can understand some learned or memorized words and phrases when I read.

Q20

I can recognize words, phrases, and characters with the help of visuals.

Q21

I can recognize words, phrases, and characters when I associate them with things I already know.
