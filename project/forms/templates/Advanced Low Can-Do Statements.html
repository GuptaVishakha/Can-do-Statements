Advanced Low Can-Do Statements

59 Points

The NCSSFL-ACTFL Can-Do Statements are self-assessment checklists used by language learners to assess what they “can do” with language in the Interpersonal, Interpretive, and Presentational modes of communication. Ultimately, the goal for all language learners is to develop a functional use of another language for one’s personal contexts and purposes. Use the checklist that will follow in a series of quizzes to record what you think you can do in the target language. You may realize that your progression may not be the same for each mode: Interpersonal, Interpretive, or Presentational. This is to be completely expected. For example, you may progress more quickly in Interpretive Reading than in Interpersonal Communication. To begin, you'll start the process with the lowest level of proficiency and work your way up from there. Make sure to be honest with yourself so you can best assess your own abilities in your target language.

Interpersonal Communication: Advanced Low

I can participate in conversations about familiar topics that go beyond my everyday life. I can talk in an organized way and with some detail about events and experiences in various time frames. I can describe people, places, and things in an organized way and with some detail. I can handle a familiar situation with an unexpected complication.

Q1

I can participate in conversations on a wide variety of topics that go beyond my everyday life.

Q2

I can compare and contrast life in different locations and in different times.

Q3

I can resolve an unexpected complication that arises in a familiar situation.

Q4

I can conduct or participate in interviews.

Presentational Speaking: Advanced Low

I can deliver organized presentations appropriate to my audience on a variety of topics. I can present information about events and experiences in various time frames.

Q5

I can deliver short presentations on a number of academic and workplace topics.

Q6

I can present my point of view and provide reasons to support it.

Q7

I can explain issues of public and community interest, including different viewpoints.

Q8

I can deliver presentations for a specific audience.

Presentational Writing: Advanced Low

I can write on general interest, academic, and professional topics. I can write organized paragraphs about events and experiences in various time frames.

Q9

I can meet basic school and academic writing needs.

Q10

I can meet basic work and career writing needs.

Q11

I can meet basic social and civic writing needs.

Interpretive Listening: Advanced Low

I can easily understand the main idea in messages and presentations on a variety I can understand the main idea and some supporting details in organized speech on a variety of topics of personal and general interest. I can follow stories and descriptions of some length and in various time frames. I can understand information presented in a variety of genres on familiar topics, even when something unexpected is expressed.

Q12

I can understand descriptions and stories of events that have happened or will happen.

Q13

I can understand the main idea of popular genres.

Interpretive Reading: Advanced Low

I can understand the main idea and some supporting details on a variety of topics of personal and general interest. I can follow stories and descriptions of some length and in various time frames and genres.

Q14

I can find and use information for practical purposes.

Q15

I can read texts that compare and contrast information.

Q16

I can follow simple written instructions.
