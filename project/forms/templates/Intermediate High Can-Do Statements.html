Intermediate High Can-Do Statements

60 Points

The NCSSFL-ACTFL Can-Do Statements are self-assessment checklists used by language learners to assess what they “can do” with language in the Interpersonal, Interpretive, and Presentational modes of communication. Ultimately, the goal for all language learners is to develop a functional use of another language for one’s personal contexts and purposes. Use the checklist that will follow in a series of quizzes to record what you think you can do in the target language. You may realize that your progression may not be the same for each mode: Interpersonal, Interpretive, or Presentational. This is to be completely expected. For example, you may progress more quickly in Interpretive Reading than in Interpersonal Communication. To begin, you'll start the process with the lowest level of proficiency and work your way up from there. Make sure to be honest with yourself so you can best assess your own abilities in your target language.

Interpersonal Communication: Intermediate High

I can participate with ease and confidence in conversations on familiar topics. I can usually talk about events and experiences in various time frames. I can usually describe people, places, and things. I can handle social interactions in everyday situations, sometimes even when there is an unexpected complication.

Q1

I can exchange information related to areas of mutual interest.

Q2

I can use my language to do a task that requires multiple steps.

Q3

I can use my language to handle a situation that may have a complication.

I can usually describe people, places, and things. I can handle social interactions in everyday situations, sometimes even when there is an unexpected complication.

Q4

Q5

Q6

Presentational Writing: Intermediate High

I can write on topics related to school, work, and community in a generally organized way. I can write some simple paragraphs about events and experiences in various time frames.

Q7

I can write about school and academic topics.

Q8

I can write about work and career topics.

Q9

I can write about community topics and events.

Q10

I can write about an entertainment or social event.

Interpretive Listening: Intermediate High

I can easily understand the main idea in messages and presentations on a variety of topics related to everyday life and personal interests and studies. I can usually understand a few details of what I overhear in conversations, even when something unexpected is expressed. I can sometimes follow what I hear about events and experiences in various time frames.

Q11

I can easily understand straightforward information or interactions.

Q12

I can understand a few details in ads, announcements, and other simple recordings.

Q13

I can sometimes understand situations with complicating factors.

Interpretive Reading: Intermediate High

I can easily understand the main idea of texts related to everyday life, personal interests, and studies. I can sometimes follow stories and descriptions about events and experiences in various time frames.

Q14

I can understand accounts of personal events or experiences.

Q15

I can sometimes follow short, written instructions when supported by visuals.

Q16

I can understand the main idea of and a few supporting facts about famous people and historic events.
